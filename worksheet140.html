Coordinate Geometry

Distance Formula : To find the distance between two points P(x₁, y₁) and Q(x₂, y₂)

d = √((x₂ - x₁)² + (y₂ - y₁)²)

Section Formula :Determines the coordinates of a point that divides a line segment joining two points A(x₁, y₁) and B(x₂, y₂) in a given ratio m:n (internally).

x = (mx₂ + nx₁) / (m+n), y = (my₂ + ny₁) / (m+n)

Midpoint Formula : A special case of the section formula where the ratio is 1:1, finding the middle point of a line segment.

x = (x₁ + x₂) / 2, y = (y₁ + y₂) / 2

Area of a triangle : Deterine the area when vertices are A(x₁, y₁), B(x₂, y₂), and C(x₃, y₃).

Area = ½ |x₁(y₂ - y₃) + x₂(y₃ - y₁) + x₃(y₁ - y₂)|

*Note If the area is zero, the three points are collinear.
